OCR Translation

Translate text from images, screenshots, and graphic art

Powerful OCR technology extracts and translates text from any image, screenshot, or graphic design. Works with photos, memes, game UI, infographics, and more. Also supports traditional text highlighting — double-tap Ctrl on selected text for instant translation.

Multi-Language Support

Chinese, Japanese, and Korean today — every language soon

Specialized support for Chinese, Japanese, and Korean with accurate character recognition and pronunciation guides. Lock your source language for consistent translations, or use auto-detect for flexibility. Advanced romanization with tone marks for Chinese, macrons for Japanese, and proper transliteration for Korean. Additional languages are already in development.

Works Everywhere

Discord, PDFs, games, browsers — translate from any application

System-level integration means Languaro works in every application on your computer. Chat apps, document readers, video games, web browsers, design tools — if you can see text, you can translate it. No browser extensions or app-specific plugins required.

Translation History

Access your past translations with keyboard shortcut

Every translation is automatically saved to your clipboard history. Press Ctrl+Shift+H to instantly view and reuse previous translations. Search through your history, copy entries, and never lose an important translation again. All stored locally on your device.

Word Breakdown

Detailed word-by-word analysis with pronunciation guides

Get granular insights into each word's meaning, grammatical role, and pronunciation. Includes proper romanization with tone marks for tonal languages, multiple definitions, and contextual usage. Perfect for language learners who want to understand structure, not just meaning.

Privacy First

Your data stays on your device. No tracking.

All translation history and preferences are stored locally on your computer. We don't collect usage data, track what you translate, or build user profiles. Your API keys stay encrypted on your machine. What you translate is nobody's business but yours.
